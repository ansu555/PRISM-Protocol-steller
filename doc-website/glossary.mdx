# Glossary

## AMM

Automated market maker. PRISM uses Soroswap constant-product pools to trade tranche tokens against USDC.

## Constant-Product

The AMM pricing rule `x * y = k`, where `x` and `y` are reserves of the two tokens in a pool and `k` is a constant. Each trade keeps the product unchanged and shifts price along the curve.

## Credit Event

An event that records a vault impairment and applies the loss cascade. The direct admin path accepts default and partial-loss events; oracle-backed handlers can also prove a default or collateral liquidation.

## Default Cascade

The reverse-priority loss application that absorbs realized losses Alpha → Core → Prime. Prime protection emerges from this ordering, not from any external guarantee.

## Alpha Tranche

The highest-risk tranche. Alpha receives yield remaining after Prime and Core fill their configured targets, and it absorbs losses first.

## Fixed-Point (Q64.64)

Rational-number representation used for NAV math. Q64.64 stores values as 128-bit integers where the high 64 bits are the integer part and the low 64 bits are the fractional part. Avoids floating-point drift and gives deterministic on-chain accounting.

## Liquidity Provider (LP)

An account that deposits both sides of an AMM pool (a tranche token and USDC) and receives LP tokens representing a proportional share of pool reserves and accumulated fees.

## Loss Bucket

An internal accounting balance maintained by `prism_core`. When a credit event reduces tranche assets, the contract increments this balance by the realized loss while keeping the corresponding USDC in the contract.

## Core Tranche

The intermediate tranche. Core is paid after Prime and absorbs losses after Alpha.

## NAV

Net asset value per share.

```text
NAV = total_assets / total_supply
```

## Contract Authority

On Soroban, the `prism_core` contract address holds and moves the vault USDC reserve and mints and burns tranche tokens. Privileged flows require configured admin authentication, but reserve transfers remain enforced by contract code.

## pALPHA

Soroban token representing a claim on the Alpha tranche.

## pCORE

Soroban token representing a claim on the Core tranche.

## pPRIME

Soroban token representing a claim on the Prime tranche.

## PRISM

**P**rogrammable **R**isk and **I**ncome **S**tructured **M**arkets. The protocol decomposes a credit pool into tranches the same way a prism separates light into bands.

## Reserve (Vault Reserve)

The USDC balance held by `prism_core`. It stores deposited USDC, incoming yield, and the amounts represented by internal loss accounting, and it sources funds for withdrawals.

## Reserve Invariant

The accounting rule that the contract USDC balance must equal tranche assets plus the internal loss-accounting balance.

```text
contract_usdc_balance = prime_assets + core_assets + alpha_assets + loss_bucket_balance
```

Maintained by incrementing `loss_bucket_balance` whenever a credit event reduces tranche assets.

## Prime Tranche

The most protected tranche. Prime is paid first and absorbs losses last.

## Strategy Preset

An interface-level allocation template (Conservative, Balanced, Aggressive) that splits a single deposit across the three tranches according to a fixed risk profile. Presets are user-experience tools, not protocol primitives.

## Subordination

The hierarchy that determines which tranche absorbs losses first. Alpha is most subordinated, Prime is least.

## Tranche

A risk-segmented slice of a credit vault. Each tranche has its own NAV, target yield, loss position, and tranche token.

## Vault

A single PRISM credit pool. Contains a USDC reserve, three tranches, optional loan references, and a credit-event log. Vault state machine: `Active`, `Defaulted`, `Resolved`.

## Waterfall

The deterministic payment order used to distribute incoming yield. Prime fills its configured target first, Core fills its configured target second, and Alpha receives the remaining yield.
